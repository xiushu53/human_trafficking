Exploring the human trafficking network

Exploring the international network structure of human trafficking from CTDC.

CTDC

The Counter-Trafficking Data Collaborative (CTDC) is the global data hub on human trafficking, with data contributed by counter-trafficking organizations around the world.

Data Source: CTDC

The data currently has 47102 entities. They are the situation of the victim reported through case management and hotline.

The right graph shows the relationship between citizenship and exploiting countries of the victims included in each entity. The area of each circle is proportional to the number of victims in that country. The thickness of edge is the number of victims involved in both countries.

1. Human trafficking has the international network

The network group is roughly divided into three groups.

  1. Asia-Pcific group
  2. Europe group
  3. Senegal group

Fortunately there is no network group with a global spread. However, the existence of a network hub hidden in the darkness that connects Europe and Asia Pacific can not be denied.

2. Network group composition

Each network group consists of four category countries.

  1. Supplier: The countries that there is no exploitation in the domestic but nationals are exploited abroad
  2. Exploiter: Countries where the foreign victims are being exploited
  3. Relay hub: Countries that are both supplier and exploiter
  4. Domestic: Countries that all victims are their citizens

To see the composition of each network group, please select "Network group" on the right side pull down menu.

3. Relationship between supplier and exploiter

Supplier countries are often developing countries, exploiter countries are often developed countries.

It may be possible to reduce economic and cultural factors by improving human development indicators.
